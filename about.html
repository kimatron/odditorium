About The Odditorium

Where weird is wonderful and strange is celebrated

Our Story

The Odditorium began when a group of eccentric artisans realized their creations were "too weird" for traditional galleries. Instead of conforming, they decided to celebrate the bizarre.

What started as a small collective of five artists making jewellery from shed cat whiskers has grown into a thriving community of creators who work with unconventional materials. We believe that art doesn't have to be normal to be beautiful.

Every piece in our collection tells a story. Whether it's pottery glazed with preserved tears or sculptures carved from ethically sourced wisdom teeth, our artisans pour their creativity into making truly one-of-a-kind items.

Meet Some of Our Artisans

Dr. Molar's Oddities

Former dentist turned tooth artist. Specializes in jewellery and sculptures made from ethically sourced wisdom teeth. "Every tooth tells a story," they say.

Feline Finds Studio

Creates beautiful items from naturally shed cat whiskers and fur. All materials are collected from willing feline donors (their own cats) over many years.

Emotional Alchemy

Preserves tears in glass vials and incorporates them into pottery glazes. Each piece captures a specific emotion - joy, sadness, or laughter.

The Keratin Collective

Transforms nail clippings into intricate mosaics and resin art. What most people throw away, they turn into stunning decorative pieces.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are all the products really handmade?

Yes! Every single item on our site has been handcrafted by local artisans. No mass production, no factories - each piece is unique and made with care.

Where do you source your materials?

All materials are ethically sourced with full consent. Teeth come from willing donors (usually the artists themselves or family members), cat whiskers are naturally shed, tears are collected with permission, and nail clippings are donated.

Is this... sanitary?

Absolutely! All organic materials undergo thorough cleaning and preservation processes. Items are sealed in resin or treated with preservatives to ensure they're completely hygienic.

Can I commission a custom piece?

Yes! Many of our artisans accept custom commissions. Contact us through our contact form with your idea, and we'll connect you with the right artist.

Do you ship internationally?

Currently we ship within Ireland and the UK. International shipping is being considered for the future, but some countries have restrictions on importing organic materials.

What's your return policy?

Due to the unique and personal nature of our items, all sales are final. However, if an item arrives damaged, we'll work with you to find a solution.
